Data Visualisation:

What Makes A Song Popular?

About

The purpose of this project is to analyse the audio features of top songs by popular global artists and investigate if there is a correlation between these features and their popularity. This project consists of 3 interactive visualisations to experiment with, all displayed in a music theme. These visualisations are all displaying between 4 - 9 audio features of the 10 most popular songs from each year between 2013 and 2022. This data has been gathered and the audio features are measured using Spotify API (these are Spotify API's definition of audio features). Before engaging in these visualisations, please click on each tab to read how each audio feature is measured and the importance of them in order to understand what these visualisations are showing. We hope you can learn more about how the popular music has evolved over the last 10 years.

  • How is Tempo measured?
    BMPS - Describes the timing of the music or the speed at which a piece of music is played.
  • How is Danceability measured?
    Describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of musical elements, including tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and overall regularity. A value of 0.0, is least danceable, and 1.0, is most danceable.
  • How is Energy measured?
    A measure from 0.0 to 1.0 and represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity. Typically, energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy. For example, death metal has high energy, while a Bach prelude scores low on the scale Perceptual features contributing to this attribute include dynamic ranfe, perceived koudness, timbre, onset rate and general entropy.
  • How is Instrumentalness measured?
    Represents the number of vocals in the song. the closer it is to 1.0, the greater likelihood the song contains no vocal content.
  • How is Valence measured?
    Describes the muscial positiveness conveyed by a track, with a measure from 0.0 to 1.0. Tracks with high valence sound more positive (e.g., happy, cheerful, euphoric), while tracks with low valence sound more negative (e.g. sad, depressed, angry).
  • How is Speechiness measured?
    Detects the presence of spoken words in a track. The more exclusively speech-like the recording (e.g. talk show, audio book, poetry), the closer to 1.0 the attribute value. Values above 0.66 describe tracks that are probably made entirely of spoken words. Values between 0.33 and 0.66 describe tracks that may contain both music and speech, either in sections or layered, including such cases as rap music. Values below 0.33 most likely represent music and other non-speech-like tracks.
  • How is Loudness measured?
    The overall loudness of a track in decibels (dB). Loudness values are averaged across the entire track and are useful for comparing relative loudness of tracks. Loudness is the quality of a sound that is the primary psychological correlate of physical strength (amplitude). Values typically range between -60 and 0 db.
  • How is Acoustiness measured?
    A confidence measure from 0.0 to 1.0 of whether the track is acoustic. 1.0 represents high confidence the track is acoustic.
  • Liveness
    Detects the presence of an audience in the recording. Higher liveness values represent an increased probability that the track was performed live. A value above 0.8 provides strong likelihood that the track is live.

Equaliser Visualisation

About

This graph is inspired by an equaliser and represents the top 10 most popular songs from the past 10 years. By choosing the buttons on the right, users can compare the audio features of each track, including Tempo, Danceability, Energy, and Instrumentalness. The height of the equaliser bars corresponds to the magnitude of the audio feature. For example, if Danceability is closer to 1.0, the bar will be taller.

How to Use

Click on a year button to display the top 10 tracks for that year. Data will only appear when a year button is clicked, clicking the same button again will hide the data. The default audio feature is "danceability," highlighted in white and labelled "On." To see different audio features, click on another button. Both the year and audio feature buttons must be active to show the data. Hover over a bar for detailed information on the track, artist, year, and audio features.

CD Visualisation

About

This visualisation takes inspiration from a CD disk and differs from the previous graph. Instead of displaying 10 tracks at a time, each individual bar on the CD represents each track. The tracks are displayed on the disk in chronolgoical order by data. The higher the audio feature, the darker the colour e.g, if energy is 1.0, the track will be displayed as a dark purple. This visualisation displays 9 audio features rather than 4, which allows you to look at the top tracks in more depth.

How to Use

Click on any bar desired. The bar will be highlighted as yellow and the default audio feature will be Tempo. To find out other audio features of the track, press 1-9 as listed next to the disk to see different features. To see a different track, simply click on a different bar.

Summary Visualisation

About

This graph is inspired by synthesiser sound waves and aims so show trends and conclusions about all the audio features. Each buttons shows you the calculated mean average of each year of songs and presents them in one graphs. This graph should be read after experimententing with the first two visualisations to see if you notice any similarities in audio feature over the years.

How to Use

Simply click on an audio feature you want to see data about and look to the table on the left of the graph. This tells you each value to the corresponding year.
